Some Examples

  • Sevdi Gönlüm Ey Melek Sima Seni

    • This is a vocal recording of  "Sevdi Gönlüm" by Nikoğos Ağa, the Armenian composer from 19th century Istanbul. The şarkı is sung by Hafız Kemal Bey, the head-müezzin of Süleymaniye Mosque. The performance was recorded in the start of the 20th century and shows the outstanding virtuosity of Kemal Bey. The recording starts with a brief improvisation from Yorgo Bacanos, one of the greatest performers of oud in 20th century. The Greek musician's technical virtuosity especially stands out in his fast and precise movements between the vocal sections.

    The musical quality can be attributed to the tight interaction between Hafız Kemal Bey and Yorgo Bacanos, who have made occasional collaborations with each other. The performance realized by these two musicians also marks an important point in the multi-cultural world of Istanbul in early 20th century.

  • Gel Güzelim Çamlıca'ya

    • This is a performance of Faiz Kapancı's famous composition sung by Münir Nurettin Selçuk, one of the best male vocals performing makam music. Münir Nurettin Selçuk is praised for his incredible control over the dynamics and articulation of his voice. Between 1:43 and 2:54 seconds in this recording, he also shows that he among the best gazel performers, a improvisational form/vocal section.

  • Muhayyer Sazsemaisi

    • This is a recording of Tanburi Cemil Bey's sazsemaisi in Muhayyer makam performed solo by the oud performer, Necati Çelik. The performer approaches the music with a fast paced and embellished manner. His performance incorporate many note repetitions and additions; He also uses inserts many bass lines, small motifs and unnotated scales. This piece is particularly hard (if conceptually possible) to side-read the score in the note-level. Nevertheless, our alignment algorithm is able to track the score except the 4th Hane between 2:46 and 3:15, where the section is performed in much faster than the notated tempo. Similarly, it cannot adapt to the abrupt tempo change in the end of the last Teslim performed.

  • Uşşâk Saz Semâîsi

    • Here, we listen to one of the best contemporary collaborations, between Murat Aydemir & Derya Türkan. Arguably the best living performers of tanbur and kemençe, they realize one of the most carefully thought performances of Neyzen Salih Dede's timeless composition. Apart from Derya Türkan's signature tone and Murat Aydemir's evident plectrum technique, the performance conveys some of the most brilliant heterophonic interactions. Focusing on the 4th Hane, one can easily spot the richness such expressivity. Here you can also observe that the predominant melody extraction is switching between two instruments, hence have many octave errors. Here, we are easily fixing the pitch heights by referring to the aligned note.

  • Pençgah Solo

    • In this recording Niyazi Sayin, one of the grandmasters of ney, performs 4 taksims and 3 compositions simultaneously. During the performance he uses the same ney, which implies the same ahenk (transposition). Until the start of the 3rd taksim, the tonic is performed approximately at 403 Hz. It shifts to 362 Hz within the performance of 3rd Taksim and stays the same in the performance of Acemaşiran Peşrev (1492 to 1773 seconds). In this example we are focusing on the performance of Acemaşiran Peşrev, the last performance. Here we want to demonstrate that, 1) our system is able to match the tonic of a subperformance, 2) our system is able to align subperformances.
